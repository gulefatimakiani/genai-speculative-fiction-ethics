# Ethics of Justice
The “ethics of justice” consists of an ethical perspective that draws on universal principles and rules that are impartial and verifiable to ensure the fair and equitable treatment of all in individuals (Botes, 1998; Edwards, 2009; Kohlberg, 1981). These ethical perspectives prioritize an ideal of objective decision-making whereby impartial individuals in power act with autonomy to distribute, enact, or disrupt systems that impact the broader society (Edwards, 2009).
