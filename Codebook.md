# Ethics of Justice

The “ethics of justice” consists of an ethical perspective that draws on universal principles and rules that are impartial and verifiable to ensure the fair and equitable treatment of all in individuals (Botes, 1998; Edwards, 2009; Kohlberg, 1981). These ethical perspectives prioritize an ideal of objective decision-making whereby impartial individuals in power act with autonomy to distribute, enact, or disrupt systems that impact the broader society (Edwards, 2009).

# Ethics of Care

Rooted in the feminist ethical tradition and originated by Carol Gilligan, the ethics of care prioritizes relational issues, involvement, harmony, contextualization, and importantly, emotions (Flanagan & Jackson, 1987; Gilligan, 1993; Wiseman, 1996). This is in contrast with highly rational and technocratic viewpoints at the expense of emotions, which are viewed as irrelevant or even detrimental to good decision making (Lönngren et al., 2021). However, there is broad recognition that emotions play a role in developing empathy for end users, which has gained increased importance in the user-centered design curriculum (Hess et al., 2021).
